Cities Facts

Tokyo

The capital of Japan, the center of the Greater Tokyo Area, and the most populous metropolitan area in the world. Tokyo has the most Michelin starred restaurants worldwide. Tokyo was originally a small fishing village named Edo, now it has the largest metropolitan economy in the world.

A typical size of a one bedroom apartment is usually not more than 15 square meters.

Canton

Also known as Guangzhou, the city has more restaurants and teahouses than any other city in China. It is the political, economic, sci-tech, educational and cultural hub of southern China. The city lies close to the South China Sea, Hong Kong and Macau.

Shanghai

Translates literally as 'Above the Sea'. Shanghai is a port city on the Huangpu River.

After the 1842 Treaty of Nanking, the British, French, Americans, Germans, and Russians moved in, setting up their distinct Western-style banks, trading houses, and mansions. The city flourished as a cosmopolitan and thriving commercial and financial center, dubbed the "Pairs of the East" in 1920s and 1930s.

Jakarta

The capital of Indonesia, the world’s largest Muslim country. Jakarta sprawls over a 637.44 square km area. Jakarta is the world's most active twitter city. The city got Independence in 1945 from Dutch East India Company.

Seoul

The capital and largest city in South Korea. In Seoul, most buildings do not have a fourth floor as 4 is considered unlucky. Seoul has been continuously settled for over 2,000 years

Seoul has become the headquarters for many international companies. Currently it is the headquarters of companies like Samsung, LG, Hyundai and Kia. The city also generates over 20% of South Korea's gross domestic product.

Delhi

Capital of modern India since 1911, Delhi has some of the most captivating old monuments, engaging art and culture, and modern marvels, such as, the Delhi Metro. The city was destroyed and rebuilt seven times.

Hindi, English, Punjabi, and Urdu are the most common languages of Delhi.

Mumbai

The city is built on what was once a group of seven islands. It was previously known as Bombay. The Mumbai suburban trains or locals, the lifeline of the city, carry more than 7.2 million commuters’ everyday. The Mumbai suburban trains carry nearly 200,000 lunch boxes known as dabbas everyday which are distributed by the 4000 odd “Dabbawalas” to corporates, workers and students alike.

In Mumbai the high court system has a waiting list of 20 years.

New York

NYC has the largest Chinese population of any city outside of Asia. It can cost over $289,000 for a one-year hot dog stand permit in Central Park.

The Federal Reserve Bank on New York’s Wall Street contains vaults that are located 80 feet beneath the bank and hold about 25 percent of the world’s gold bullion.

Albert Einstein’s eyeballs are stored in a safe deposit box in the city.
